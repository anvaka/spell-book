M01
All warfare is deception ↔ Disinformation & false-flag cyber ops
▸
Sun Tzŭ · ancient
Ch. I — Laying Plans, §§ 18–24

All warfare is based on deception. Hence, when able to attack, we must seem unable; when using our forces, we must seem inactive; when we are near, we must make the enemy believe we are far away; when far away, we must make him believe we are near… Attack him where he is unprepared, appear where you are not expected.

↔
// modern doctrine

Qiao Liang & Wang Xiangsui, Unrestricted Warfare (PLA Literature and Arts Publishing House, Beijing 1999) opens with Sun Tzŭ and argues that the battlefield is "everywhere" — financial, legal, network, media — and that the first principle is still deception, now fought with news cycles and packets rather than banners.

Valery Gerasimov, "The Value of Science in Prediction," Voyenno-Promyshlennyy Kuryer (Military-Industrial Courier), 27 Feb 2013, argues that non-military means — including information operations and economic pressure — have become decisive in modern conflict, and that the lines between war and peace have blurred — a claim routinely glossed by Western analysts as a Sun-Tzŭian inversion of Clausewitz.

> Qiao & Wang 1999, ch. 2 · Gerasimov 2013 · tr. Charles K. Bartles, Military Review Jan–Feb 2016
case vignette

The "Guccifer 2.0" false-flag — DNC leak, 2016

June 2016 · attributed to GRU Units 26165 / 74455 (APT28 · "Fancy Bear")

After Russian military intelligence exfiltrated the DNC's email archives, the leaks were released through a persona ("Guccifer 2.0") posing as a lone Romanian hacker, and through a front site ("DCLeaks"). The goal was not merely to steal — it was to seem to be someone else while striking. Sun Tzŭ's injunction to "seem unable" while able, and to appear where one is not expected, is recognizable line-for-line.

Mueller, Report on the Investigation into Russian Interference in the 2016 Presidential Election, Vol. I, pp. 36–50 (DOJ, March 2019); U.S. v. Netyksho et al., indictment, D.D.C., 13 July 2018.

M02
Foreknowledge from other men ↔ All-source intelligence & I&W
▸
Sun Tzŭ · ancient
Ch. XIII — The Use of Spies, §§ 4–6

What enables the wise sovereign and the good general to strike and conquer, and achieve things beyond the reach of ordinary men, is foreknowledge… this foreknowledge cannot be elicited from spirits; it cannot be obtained inductively from experience, nor by any deductive calculation. Knowledge of the enemy's dispositions can only be obtained from other men.

↔
// modern doctrine

Michael Warner, CIA Historian, "Wanted: A Definition of 'Intelligence,'" Studies in Intelligence 46 no. 3 (2002), opens by quoting Sun Tzŭ's "foreknowledge" paragraph as the oldest surviving theory of intelligence as a distinct craft — and notes that the modern all-source discipline is essentially the same problem re-stated.

U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff, JP 2-0 Joint Intelligence (22 Oct 2013), §I-1, opens its historical framing with the same verse, calling it "the enduring principle" underlying indications & warning. It is the most-quoted sentence of Sun Tzŭ in Western military doctrine.

> Warner 2002, Studies in Intelligence 46(3) · JP 2-0 (2013) ch. I
case vignette

SolarWinds — supply-chain foreknowledge, 2019–2020

discovered Dec 2020 · attributed to SVR (APT29 · "Cozy Bear")

Russian operators trojanized updates to the Orion IT-management platform, gaining silent, long-dwell visibility into ≈18,000 customer networks including the U.S. Departments of Treasury, Commerce, State, Energy and DHS. The campaign was pure foreknowledge: not destruction, but "knowledge of the enemy's dispositions" on a continental scale — the ideal form of Sun Tzŭ's inward spy expressed as a software update.

CISA Alert AA20-352A, "Advanced Persistent Threat Compromise of Government Agencies, Critical Infrastructure, and Private Sector Organizations" (17 Dec 2020); Microsoft MSRC, "Customer Guidance on Recent Nation-State Cyber Attacks" (13 Dec 2020); ODNI joint statement (5 Jan 2021).

M03
The five classes of spies ↔ HUMINT · SIGINT · APT implants · insiders
▸
Sun Tzŭ · ancient
Ch. XIII — The Use of Spies, §§ 7–8

Hence the use of spies, of whom there are five classes: (1) Local spies; (2) inward spies; (3) converted spies; (4) doomed spies; (5) surviving spies. When these five kinds of spy are all at work, none can discover the secret system. This is called "divine manipulation of the threads." It is the sovereign's most precious faculty.

↔
// modern doctrine

Mandiant, APT1: Exposing One of China's Cyber Espionage Units (Alexandria VA, 19 Feb 2013), documents PLA Unit 61398's seven-year campaign against 141 organizations across 20 sectors. Industry analysts have long read Unit 61398's tradecraft — long-dwell implants, credential theft, quiet exfiltration — as the closest modern equivalent of Sun Tzŭ's inward and local spies, rendered in malware.

Ralph D. Sawyer, The Tao of Spycraft (Westview Press 1998) is the standard Western scholarly treatment of Chinese covert-action literature and devotes its opening chapters to Sun Tzŭ's five-spy taxonomy as the source-text of the entire Chinese tradition of secret warfare.

> Mandiant/FireEye 2013 · Sawyer 1998 · DoD, Annual Report to Congress: Military & Security Developments Involving the PRC
case vignette

APT1 / PLA Unit 61398 — Shanghai, 2006–2013

Mandiant report released 19 February 2013

Mandiant's 74-page report traced 141 confirmed intrusions, an average dwell time of 356 days, and a single building in Shanghai's Pudong district as the physical home of the operators. The tradecraft reads as a literal implementation of Sun Tzŭ's chapter XIII: inward spies (stolen credentials inside target firms), local spies (compromised domestic infrastructure used as hop-points), and surviving spies (long-lived implants reporting back).

Mandiant, APT1 (2013); DOJ indictment U.S. v. Wang Dong et al., W.D. Pa., 19 May 2014 (first U.S. charges against uniformed PLA officers for cyber espionage).

verse trace — closest anticipations
Having local spies means employing the services of the inhabitants of a district. Having inward spies, making use of officials of the enemy. Having converted spies, getting hold of the enemy's spies and using them for our own purposes. Ch. XIII · §§ 9–11
Be subtle! be subtle! and use your spies for every kind of business. Ch. XIII · §18
M04
Attack by fire ↔ Cyber sabotage of ICS / critical infrastructure
▸
Sun Tzŭ · ancient
Ch. XII — The Attack by Fire, §§ 1–3, 13

There are five ways of attacking with fire. The first is to burn soldiers in their camp; the second is to burn stores; the third is to burn baggage-trains; the fourth is to burn arsenals and magazines; the fifth is to hurl dropping fire amongst the enemy… There is a proper season for making attacks with fire, and special days for starting a conflagration… Hence those who use fire as an aid to the attack show intelligence.

↔
// modern doctrine

RAND, Cyber Warfare: How Conflicts in Cyberspace Are Challenging America and Changing the World (Paul Cornish et al., Chatham House / RAND commentary 2010–2013) frames cyber attacks on industrial control systems as the logical modern heir of attack-by-fire: a means of destroying the enemy's physical stores and baggage-trains without armies ever meeting.

Martin C. Libicki (RAND), Conquest in Cyberspace: National Security and Information Warfare (Cambridge UP / RAND 2007), argues that cyber operations against infrastructure are a "weapon of mass disruption" and repeatedly invokes Sun Tzŭ on the seasonality and preparation of fire as analogous to the need for pre-positioned access.

> Libicki 2007 (RAND) · Cornish et al. 2010 · U.S. DoD Cyber Strategy 2018
case vignette

Stuxnet — Natanz centrifuge hall, 2007–2010

Discovered June 2010 · "Operation Olympic Games"

A worm of unprecedented sophistication crossed an air gap via USB, targeted Siemens S7-300 PLCs driving IR-1 uranium centrifuges at Natanz, and silently drove them to destruction while reporting normal telemetry. Roughly a thousand centrifuges were physically wrecked. This is Sun Tzŭ's fourth category — to burn arsenals and magazines — fulfilled by a few kilobytes of code. The "proper season" and "means available" became years of stolen digital certificates, stolen SCADA documentation, and four zero-days.

Ralph Langner, "To Kill a Centrifuge" (Langner Communications, Nov 2013); Symantec, W32.Stuxnet Dossier v1.4 (Feb 2011); David E. Sanger, Confront and Conceal (Crown 2012) ch. 8, "Olympic Games."

case vignette

NotPetya — Ukraine & global spillover, 2017

27 June 2017 · attributed to GRU Unit 74455 (Sandworm)

A destructive wiper masquerading as ransomware, pushed through the M.E.Doc Ukrainian tax-software update channel, crippled Maersk, Merck, FedEx/TNT, Mondelēz, and Ukrainian ministries, banks, and the Chernobyl radiation-monitoring system. Damages exceeded US$10 billion — the most destructive cyber-attack in history. An almost perfect modern instance of "hurl[ing] dropping fire amongst the enemy" with a supply-chain torch.

Andy Greenberg, Sandworm (Doubleday 2019); White House statement, 15 Feb 2018; UK NCSC attribution statement, 15 Feb 2018.

M05
Subdue the enemy without fighting ↔ Gray-zone coercion & hybrid warfare
▸
Sun Tzŭ · ancient
Ch. III — Attack by Stratagem, §§ 2, 6

Hence to fight and conquer in all your battles is not supreme excellence; supreme excellence consists in breaking the enemy's resistance without fighting… Therefore the skilful leader subdues the enemy's troops without any fighting; he captures their cities without laying siege to them; he overthrows their kingdom without lengthy operations in the field.

↔
// modern doctrine

NATO, Strategic Concept 2022 (adopted Madrid, 29 June 2022), para. 27, formally recognises "hybrid operations against Allies" as a potential Article 5 trigger, framing an adversary space below the threshold of open war. Alliance analysts routinely trace the conceptual ancestry of this gray-zone framing to Sun Tzŭ's "supreme excellence" passage.

Frank Hoffman, Conflict in the 21st Century: The Rise of Hybrid Wars (Potomac Institute, Dec 2007), the field-defining monograph on "hybrid warfare," cites Sun Tzŭ explicitly for the principle of achieving strategic ends through non-kinetic means.

RAND, Russian Social Media Influence (Helmus, Bodine-Baron, et al., RR-2237-OSD, 2018), describes Russian information operations as a "whole-of-society" effort to shape target populations before any conflict — an explicit Sun-Tzŭian framing of winning before the war begins.

> NATO Strategic Concept 2022 · Hoffman 2007 · RAND RR-2237 (2018)
case vignette

Russian interference in the 2016 U.S. election

2014–2017 · Internet Research Agency (St. Petersburg) + GRU

A combined covert influence operation — paid trolls, fabricated Facebook/Instagram personas, stolen and weaponised email archives — aimed not at seizing territory but at sowing discord, degrading institutional legitimacy, and shaping a voting population's perceptions without firing a shot. The purest modern demonstration of "breaking the enemy's resistance without fighting."

U.S. Senate Select Committee on Intelligence, Report on Russian Active Measures Campaigns and Interference in the 2016 U.S. Election, Vols. I–V (2019–2020); ODNI ICA 2017-01D (6 Jan 2017); indictments in U.S. v. Internet Research Agency, D.D.C., 16 Feb 2018.

M06
Baulk the enemy's plans ↔ Pre-emption, supply-chain implants, cognitive denial
▸
Sun Tzŭ · ancient
Ch. III — Attack by Stratagem, §3

Thus the highest form of generalship is to baulk the enemy's plans; the next best is to prevent the junction of the enemy's forces; the next in order is to attack the enemy's army in the field; and the worst policy of all is to besiege walled cities.

↔
// modern doctrine

U.S. Cyber Command, Achieve and Maintain Cyberspace Superiority: Command Vision for US Cyber Command (April 2018), codifies the "defend forward" and "persistent engagement" doctrine — continuous disruption of adversary operations short of war — which analysts such as Michael Sulmeyer (Belfer Center 2018) have read as the "baulk-the-plans" doctrine translated into a cyber idiom.

PLA Academy of Military Science, The Science of Military Strategy (战略学, 2013 edition, English excerpts in Joe McReynolds ed., China's Evolving Military Strategy, Jamestown 2017) devotes substantial space to "informationized" pre-war operations and to Sun Tzŭ's dictum on baulking the plans of the enemy as the highest form of war.

> USCYBERCOM Command Vision 2018 · SMS 2013 · McReynolds ed. 2017
case vignette

Supply-chain pre-positioning — Volt Typhoon, 2023

First public report 24 May 2023 · PRC state-linked

Microsoft and the Five Eyes cybersecurity agencies disclosed a PRC-linked actor ("Volt Typhoon") that had quietly pre-positioned itself in U.S. critical-infrastructure networks — communications, manufacturing, water, transportation — using "living-off-the-land" techniques designed to enable disruption in the event of a future crisis. It is pure Sun-Tzŭian plan-baulking: the point of access is not the exfiltration but the option value it gives during the next confrontation.

Microsoft Threat Intelligence, "Volt Typhoon targets US critical infrastructure with living-off-the-land techniques" (24 May 2023); CISA joint advisory AA23-144A (24 May 2023); follow-up AA24-038A (7 Feb 2024).
